Hackjam Projects

Synaptech hosts hackjams, single-day hackathons, to help provide students with the opportunity to get introduced to the neurotechnology field. Bi-quarterly, Synaptech will host a workshop centered on a theme. Following this workshop Synaptech will host a hackjam encouraging students to pick projects along the same theme. Students will spend the day working with a team and the support of neurotech-experienced mentors with an opportunity to win some prizes at the end of the night!

The goal of each hackjam is not to complete a project in a single day, but rather draft a complete proposal/plan or get as far as possible within the time alotted. Completed hackjam projects are collected and displayed below.

Don't Blink

Don't Blink! is a horror game that uses the Muse 2 heatset to measure the user's blinks and tension to affect gameplay. Whenever the user blinks in real life, the "weeping angel" statue within the game will appear closer and closer to them. It can also move when not on camera in-game. This combination of XR and traditional gameplay makes for a truly terrifying experience!

Blink Chase

A 2D Unity game in which the behavior of an NPC agent is changed based on the player’s blinking, as detected by the muse headset. The game combines classical video game controls and blink-to-keypress, which is similar to the implementation outlined in the Chromedino demo. The blink detection is improved by a 5-second calibration period at the start of the program.

  • Matthew

BlinkBall

BlinkBall is a baseball arcade game, where the objective is to hit the ball into a designated area for a homerun. To swing, blink. If the ball gets into the "brain area" a light will light up to indicate a homerun.
